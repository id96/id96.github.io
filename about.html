About

Isabelle is a Senior at Cornell University, where she is studying Information Science and French. Within Information Science, Isabelle is concentrating in Data Science.

Isabelle's blog, Computational Revolution, was inspired by her mentors, Professor David Mimno and PhD Candidate Laure Thompson. She took a course with them in Fall 2017, Text Mining for History and Literature, where she learned the basic tools necessary for this project and where she first discovered her appreciation for Digital Humanism. Isabelle is so lucky to have found a project that so perfectly blends her two fields of study.

At Cornell, Isabelle is the President of the Cornell Democrats. She is passionate about women's reproductive rights, environmental issues, and generally principles of economic/political/social justice.

Last spring, Isabelle studied abroad in Paris, where she studied Philosophy and Art History at La Sorbonne. During this semester, it was the 50th anniversary of the Mai 68 Paris Student Revolutions. Isabelle experienced manifestations, protests, and revolution herself at this time. This project on Revolution is fitting as Isabelle is interested in how Revolutions shape our lives. Isabelle also took a course in Fall 2017 with Camille Robcis and Jason Frank, Revolution, which emphasized the nuances of the French Revolution and was Isabelle's first introduction to the material used in this project (parliamentary archives of the National Assembl√©e).

Isabelle spent the past summer as a Market Research & UX Intern at Check Point Software Technologies in Tel Aviv. She had the best summer of her life doing what she loved - working in high tech during the day and exploring the city at night.
